Active Derivatives / numerical analysis Solo · 2026

Binomial Option Pricing: Convergence, Oscillation, and Strike Alignment

CRR, Tian (1999), and Leisen–Reimer compared against Black–Scholes — both empirically and on paper.

A self-study research note on binomial option pricing. The math (CRR convergence via Lindeberg–Feller, the oscillation mechanism, strike-alignment in Tian 1999) is standard and not novel — but the note is mine, the implementations are mine, and the proofs are written from first principles. Empirical convergence rates fitted on log|ε_N|: CRR β = 1.000, Tian (1999) β = 1.005, Leisen–Reimer β = 1.995 — matching the theoretical predictions to three decimals.

The repository includes a ~35-page typeset mathematical note in LaTeX, a separate ~30-page document of handwritten derivations covering replication, Lindeberg–Feller verification, and the oscillation mechanism, and 127 tests — 28 of which reproduce numerical values from Tian (1999) Tables I and II to 4–6 decimal places. The mathematical work was reviewed by Prof. Jens Lorenz (UNM Math).

Planned Statistical arbitrage / risk modeling Solo · Summer 2026

An Empirical Study of Covariance Matrix Denoising for Statistical Arbitrage

When does denoising actually help, and which estimator wins under which conditions?

The sample covariance matrix is the wrong input for any portfolio with more names than observations, but the literature is split on what to replace it with — Ledoit–Wolf linear shrinkage, nonlinear shrinkage, Marchenko–Pastur eigenvalue clipping, factor-model residuals, graphical lasso. The research question is when each estimator actually improves out-of-sample statistical-arbitrage performance, and what conditions (universe size, sample window, signal type) determine the answer.

Active Cross-sectional L/S equity Python · cvxpy · WRDS

Axiom Fund — Systematic Market-Neutral L/S Research Engine

A 4-signal composite backtested on the CRSP top-1000 universe.

Composite of gross profitability, idiosyncratic volatility, residual momentum, and post-earnings-announcement drift. Sector-, beta-, and dollar-neutral convex MVO (cvxpy) with Ledoit–Wolf shrunk covariance and Compustat-anchored point-in-time discipline. Costs modeled as commission, Corwin–Schultz half-spread, square-root impact (Almgren et al. 2005), and borrow.

Per-signal IC analysis over 116 monthly periods finds PEAD with the strongest t-statistic despite the smallest mean IC (t = 2.67, hit rate 63.8%), while residual momentum has effectively decayed (t = 0.26, p = 0.80) — consistent with the post-publication factor decay documented in McLean–Pontiff (2016). Regime decomposition shows the gross Sharpe is primarily delivered by bull-regime exposure, with IVol exhibiting the classic low-vol cyclicality (bull t = +3.83, bear t = −2.47).

Active Event-driven / single name Python · yfinance

BeatScore — Earnings Event Backtester

An 11-feature composite ranking pre-earnings setups, with cross-sectional bucketing and attribution.

For each earnings event, the system pulls a snapshot of eleven pre-announcement signals — momentum (20D, 60D), the four-quarter trailing surprise and beat rate, gross-margin trend, sector momentum, valuation (trailing P/E, P/S), implied-vol percentile, analyst-revision z-score, and short interest — and combines them into a standardized BeatScore. Cross-sections are pooled within a configurable window, terciled, and held over T+1, T+5, and T+20.

Feature importance is measured two ways: univariate correlations and a standardized linear regression on returns. Per-ticker attribution decomposes the High-minus-Low spread by name, which is where most of the interesting failure modes live.

VIX Regime-Switching Strategy

A lagged-signal three-state allocation between defensive, aggressive, and cash.

Daily rebalance between a high-quality dividend ETF (RWL), a momentum ETF (SPMO), and cash, driven by the prior day's VIX level. The lagged signal is the point: it forces the strategy to be implementable in real time and removes the common look-ahead artifact that flatters regime-switch backtests. Drawdowns compared head-to-head against the S&P 500 over 2010–2024.

Active Intraday event-driven Python · 1-minute bars

Volume-Spike Event Backtester

Minute-bar volume z-score signals with realistic execution assumptions.

An event-driven framework on 1-minute bars. The signal is a rolling volume z-score over a configurable lookback; entries are taken on the next minute's open and exited at the same minute's close, with slippage applied to both sides. The framework sweeps multiple sigma thresholds and reports trade-level statistics — win rate, mean and median per-trade return, equity curve, signal-time markers on the volume series — so the question of whether the edge is real, or just survives at one threshold, is answerable rather than asserted.

The motivation is companion work for a queue-reactive LOB simulator I'm building in C++ over the summer; this is the higher-level event layer.
